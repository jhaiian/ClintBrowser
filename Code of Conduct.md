# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want ClintBrowser to be a welcoming project for everyone, regardless of experience level, background, or identity. As contributors and maintainers, we pledge to make participation in this project through issues, pull requests, discussions, or any other project space a harassment free experience for all.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that helps build a positive environment:

* Being respectful and considerate in comments, reviews, and discussions
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
* Focusing on what is best for the project and its users
* Being patient with newcomers and first time contributors

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

* Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments, public or private
* Trolling, deliberately disruptive or inflammatory comments
* Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
* Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces including the GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, and code review) and also applies when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a private report via [GitHub's report abuse form](https://github.com/contact/report-abuse), by emailing [jhaiianbetter@duck.com](mailto:jhaiianbetter@duck.com), or by contacting the maintainer, [@jhaiian](https://github.com/jhaiian), directly through GitHub. All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate, fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful. This may include:

1. Correction  A private, written warning and clarification of why the behavior was inappropriate.
2. Warning  A warning with consequences for continued behavior, including temporary restriction from project spaces.
3. Temporary Ban  A temporary ban from any interaction or public communication with the project.
4. Permanent Ban  A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the project.
